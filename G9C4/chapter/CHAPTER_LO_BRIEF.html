Chapter Learning Objective
Given a probability experiment, students can: (1) determine the sample space using listing, tables, or tree diagrams; (2) calculate theoretical probability P(A) = n(A)/n(S) and apply the complement rule P(Aᶜ) = 1 − P(A); (3) calculate relative frequency (empirical probability) from experimental data and explain how it converges to theoretical probability as trials increase; (4) calculate expected frequency using P(A) × k; and (5) distinguish population from sample, identify representative samples, and evaluate random sampling methods — demonstrating mastery of both theoretical and empirical probability within the Kurikulum Merdeka Grade 9 scope.
Prerequisite Chain (6)
  1. Fraction arithmetic: comparing, converting between fraction/decimal/percentage forms (required for P(A) = n(A)/n(S) and relative frequency calculations)
  2. Set notation: understanding sets, subsets, and set membership using curly-bracket notation (required for defining sample space S and event A)
  3. Ratio and proportion: understanding part-to-whole relationships (required for interpreting probability as a ratio)
  4. Basic data literacy: reading bar charts, frequency tables, and understanding mean/average (required for Section B empirical probability and Section D representative samples)
  5. Number line and ordering fractions/decimals on [0,1] (required for placing probability values; activated by Exploration 4.6)
  6. Informal/intuitive likelihood language: certain, likely, unlikely, impossible (required as conceptual anchor for Exploration 4.1 and subjective probability entry point)
